"Kamrater från Kristinehams-Praktiska-skola 1917-1918"

Signe Elvira Elgh (b. 1900)

Signe Elvira Elgh was born into a family that managed Loka Brunn. She later became a hairdresser and had her own hair salon. Ever the forward-thinking woman she had a child and raised him alone, self-archived for posterity because she knew her life and experiences as an unmarried woman mattered, keeping photo memories of her life experiences and loved ones; and recording an oral herstory before her death hoping that it would share her life and times with her two grandsons.

Self-Archiving The Independent Woman

This project has relevance to the reparative LIS community; the LGBTQ for work that has been done by non-binary archivist and lesbians, and for its representation-is-not-gender approach to photo description; to the feminist community for representation of a single mother self-archiving; to the local Swedish community and historical societies; to genealogists and family historians; to the Swedish photography community and critical photo metadata community.

So What is This About?

The aim is to digitally preserve through digital scanning; to create rich metadata for the photographs; to create machine-readable metadata; to make the digitizations and metadata findable and accessible to machines and humans; to present the digital reproductions and metadata in a critical, interpretive, and aesthetically thoughtful way for human perusal.

The purpose of the project is to digitally preserve and present the photo albums of Signe Elvira Elgh b. 1900 in Molkom, Sweden; to provide the communities of Molkom, Loka, Grythyttan, Kristinehamn with an archival record of its inhabitants from the early 1900’s; to preserve the visual history of these communities; and to demonstrate critical and interpretive digitization best practices.

Project Work

GIMP was used to edit the embedded photo metadata. Types included GIMP Description, IPTC, XMP, EXIF. Metadata was exported from GIMP to XML. The metadata was also browsed using ExifTool. Interoperability between IPTC and Dublin Core is currently between Title, Subject/Keywords, Creator, Rights/Copyright Notice, and Description.
